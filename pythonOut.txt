<p><big><samp><strong>1. Estimation of Thermal Load on Rocket Base Plate</strong></samp></big></p>

<p><big><samp>The rocket base plate is heated by emission of thermal radiation from gases and particles emanating from the convergent-divergent (CD) nozzle. These gases are at high pressure, temperature, mixture of species and contains particles. Thus, accurate estimation of radiation properties of gases and particles, and also efficient solver are necessary to estimation of thermal load on rocket base plate. (<strong>MS Thesis: Estimation of Thermal Load on Rocket Base Plate From Exhaust Plume</strong>)</samp></big></p>

<p><big><samp><strong>2. Estimation of Radiation Losses/Emissions in Combustion Systems</strong></samp></big></p>

<p><big><samp>The participating species that are generated during the combustion process emit the radiation energy. This energy leaves the combustion region by radiation mode of heat transfer, i.e., loss of energy, thus causes the reduction in temperature which affects performance of the combustion system. Therefore, emission from each and every species must be accounted for the efficient working of the combustion system (<strong>MS Thesis: Estimation of Realistic Radiation Losses/Emissions in Combustion System</strong>)</samp></big></p>

<p><big><samp>3. <strong>Collimated Beam Radiation</strong></samp></big></p>

<p><big><samp>In many applications, the energy is transferred in a single direction by radiation mode of heat transfer like, solar energy incident on solar receiver cavity after reflecting from heliostat field. This energy is further traveled from the optical window of the solar receiver cavity through fluid medium to incident on the porous medium. The fluid absorbs-emits and/or scatters radiation energy before energy incident on porous medium. In another application, the solar energy incident on ocean, lakes, ponds travels in the water, gets absorb within water or reaches to the bottom, thus, affects the aquatic life. In another situation, the solar energy incident on the top clouds may affecting its dynamics. Therefore, collimated beam feature developed in our lab could be tuned to study these applications. (<strong>PhD Thesis: Interaction of Collimated Beam Radiation with Natural Convection in an Enclosure</strong>)</samp></big></p>

<p><big><samp><strong><em>1. Development of Adaptive Unstructured Angular Discretisation Grid for the Finite Volume Method of Radiative Transfer Equation for Collimated Beam Radiation</em></strong> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</samp></big></p>

<p><big><samp>It is a general myth that the radiation heat transfer is only important at very high-temperature applications, however, This is not true. In general, the radiation plays equally important role in the natural convection problems. The radiation heat transfer problems are treated in two ways (1) Surface to Surface radiation (2) Radiation in participating medium. The former way of analysis is relatively simple whereas later one is complex in all aspects, i.e.., mathematically, numerically, physics, etc. Furthermore, the radiation problems can also be categories as diffuse and collimated beam radiation. The radiation problem from elevated temperature can be treated as diffuse radiation problem, whereas radiation energy traveling in a single direction belongs to collimated beam radiation category. The radiation problems from optics to biomedical field belong to collimated beam problem. The solar radiation is also an example of collimated beam radiation.&nbsp;</samp></big></p>

<p><big><samp>The radiation heat transfer is modeled by an integrodifferential equation known as Radiative Transfer Equation (RTE). This equation represents radiation energy conservation in a direction. To achieve an accurate solution, one need to solve RTE numerically in many directions but need to limit the number of directions by keeping the computational resource in mind. Many numerical techniques have been developed to solve the RTE. The most popular technique is finite volume discrete ordinate method (FVDOM). Along with the CFD domain discretization, FVDOM also discretizes the angular domain of 4Pi. The angular domain discretization is regular using the spherical coordinate system with unit radius. This kind of angular discretization produces acceptable results for the diffuse radiation; however, getting right direction for the collimated beam radiation problems is little problematic. Thus FVDOM can not be used for the collimated beam problem in the present form. It is required to improve the angular discretization technique for the collimated beam problems. This project deals to improve the angular discretization so that FVDOM can be used with collimated beam radiation problems.</samp></big></p>
